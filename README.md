## ðŸ’¡ SECTION 2: CORE PHILOSOPHY & APPROACH

### Definition & Foundation

Predictive projects operate on the assumption that project requirements and scope can be largely understood and defined upfront. This approach relies on historical data and expert judgment to forecast project needs, timelines, and resources before execution begins.

**Key Characteristic:** The methodology is deterministicâ€”if the planning is thorough, execution should proceed predictably. This contrasts with adaptive/agile approaches which embrace uncertainty.

### Key Principles of Predictive Projects

- âœ“ **Comprehensive Planning** - All scope, schedule, cost, and risk planning done upfront
- âœ“ **Phase Gates** - Formal approval required before moving to next phase
- âœ“ **Extensive Documentation** - Detailed requirements, design specs, test plans
- âœ“ **Change Aversion** - Changes after approval require formal change control
- âœ“ **Risk Mitigation** - Risks identified and mitigated through planning
- âœ“ **Linear Dependencies** - Phases depend on completion of previous phases
- âœ“ **Single Delivery** - Complete product delivered at end of project
- âœ“ **Resource Allocation** - Different resources needed for different phases

## ðŸ’¡ SECTION 2: CORE PHILOSOPHY & APPROACH

### Definition & Foundation

Agile projects operate on the assumption that requirements, technology, and customer needs will evolve throughout the project. This approach embraces uncertainty and uses iterative delivery, frequent feedback, and continuous learning to deliver value incrementally.

**Key Characteristic:** The methodology is adaptiveâ€”if customer feedback indicates a better approach, the team pivots quickly. This contrasts with predictive projects which resist change once scope is set.

### The Four Values of the Agile Manifesto

```

1. INDIVIDUALS AND INTERACTIONS      over    Processes and tools
2. WORKING SOFTWARE                  over    Comprehensive documentation
3. CUSTOMER COLLABORATION            over    Contract negotiation
4. RESPONDING TO CHANGE              over    Following a plan

(However: the items on the right have value, we just value the left more!)
```

### Key Principles of Agile Projects

- âœ“ **Iterative Development** - Work in short cycles (sprints) with continuous refinement
- âœ“ **Incremental Delivery** - Deliver working software every sprint
- âœ“ **Continuous Planning** - Rolling-wave planning as requirements become clearer
- âœ“ **Embrace Change** - Changes are reprioritized, not rejected
- âœ“ **Customer Collaboration** - Constant feedback and involvement
- âœ“ **Cross-functional Teams** - T-shaped skills, self-organizing
- âœ“ **Built-in Quality** - Testing integrated throughout, not at end
- âœ“ **Continuous Improvement** - Retrospectives after each sprint

## â±ï¸ SECTION 3: SPRINTS & TIME-BOXING

### What is a Sprint?

A **sprint** is a fixed, consistent time box (typically 1-4 weeks) during which a development team completes a set amount of work from the product backlog and delivers a potentially shippable increment.

**Standard Sprint Duration:** 2-4 weeks (consistency is more important than exact duration)

### Sprint Ceremonies

- **Sprint Planning** - Team selects items from backlog, defines sprint goal
- **Daily Standup** - 15 minutes - What did I do? What am I doing? What's blocking me?
- **Sprint Review/Demo** - Show working software to stakeholders, gather feedback
- **Sprint Retrospective** - Team reflects on improvement opportunities

### Sprint Artifacts

| Artifact | Purpose | When Created |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| **Product Backlog** | Master list of all requirements/features prioritized by business value | Continuously evolving |
| **Sprint Backlog** | Subset of product backlog selected for current sprint | Sprint Planning |
| **Increment** | Working, tested, potentially shippable product at sprint end | Sprint completion |

## ðŸ‘¥ SECTION 4: AGILE TEAM ROLES

### Key Roles (Scrum Framework)

- **Product Owner** - Defines what to build, prioritizes backlog, accepts completed work
- **Scrum Master** - Facilitates Agile process, removes blockers, protects team
- **Development Team** - Cross-functional (5-9 people), self-organizing, delivers increment
- **Stakeholders** - Provide feedback in reviews, validate business value

### T-Shaped Skills

Team members have **deep expertise** in one area but **broad knowledge** across multiple areas. This reduces handoffs and improves collaboration.

## Top 10 Concepts to Master

1. **Iterative vs. Incremental** - Iteration = refining the same product. Increment = delivering usable chunks. Both happen in Agile; neither in Predictive.

2. **Sprint Definition** - Fixed time-box (1-4 weeks) where team delivers potentially shippable increment.

3. **Product Owner vs. Scrum Master** - PO defines WHAT; SM facilitates HOW. Never confuse these roles.

4. **Backlog Grooming** - Continuous refinement of product backlog prioritized by business value.

5. **Definition of DONE** - Clear acceptance criteria for completed work (coded, tested, reviewed, deployed).

6. **Velocity** - Story points completed per sprint; used to forecast project completion.

7. **Burn-Down Chart** - Shows work remaining vs. time; healthy chart slopes downward.

8. **Change Control in Predictive** - Changes = formal request, impact analysis, CCB approval, cost/schedule adjustment.

9. **Change in Agile** - Changes = backlog reprioritization, no formal change control needed.

10. **Retrospectives** - "What went well? What to improve? What will we commit to?" Continuous improvement engine.

## Common Exam Question Types

### Scenario Questions (Most Common)

**Format:** "A customer requests a new feature mid-project. The project manager should..."

**If PREDICTIVE:**

**If AGILE:**

### Role Questions

**Example:** "Who is responsible for the product backlog in Scrum?"

**Answer:** Product Owner. They define items, prioritize by value, refine with team.

### Progress Measurement Questions

**Example:** "How do you measure progress in Agile?"

**Answer:** Working software delivered each sprint, not documentation or intermediate milestones. Use velocity, burn-down charts, and Definition of Done.

### When to Use Which Approach

**Example:** "Which approach is best for a compliance-driven software project with fixed regulatory requirements?"

**Answer:** Predictive. Fixed requirements, low tolerance for change, extensive documentation needed.

## Practice Questions

### Question 1: Backlog Management

**Question:** Your Agile team completed 18 story points in Sprint 1 and 22 story points in Sprint 2. The product has 200 remaining story points. Approximately how many more sprints are needed?

**A)** 8 sprints | **B)** 10 sprints | **C)** 12 sprints | **D)** 15 sprints

**Answer: B) 10 sprints**

Average velocity = (18 + 22) / 2 = 20 points/sprint
Remaining work / Velocity = 200 / 20 = 10 sprints

---

### Question 2: Change Management

**Question:** In a predictive project, the sponsor requests a scope change in month 8 of a 12-month project. The new feature impacts database design. What should the PM do?

**A)** Add it to the next sprint
**B)** File formal change request, impact analysis, CCB approval
**C)** Ask team to absorb in current timeline
**D)** Defer to next project phase

**Answer: B) File formal change request, impact analysis, CCB approval**

Predictive projects use formal change control. Database design changes have cascading impacts (development, testing, schedule, budget).

---

### Question 3: Team Roles

**Question:** The development team reports that the database infrastructure is causing delays. Who should remove this blocker?

**A)** Product Owner | **B)** Scrum Master | **C)** Project Sponsor | **D)** Tech Lead

**Answer: B) Scrum Master**

Scrum Master's primary responsibility is to remove team blockers and protect team productivity. Product Owner handles "what" decisions, not operational blockers.

---

### Question 4: Continuous Improvement

**Question:** During Sprint Retrospective, the team identifies that long Daily Standups (30 minutes) are reducing coding time. What should happen next?

**A)** Eliminate standups
**B)** The Scrum Master notes the observation
**C)** Team commits to 15-minute time-box next sprint
**D)** Escalate to management

**Answer: C) Team commits to 15-minute time-box next sprint**

Retrospectives are about continuous improvement. Team should identify a specific, actionable commitment for next sprint to improve velocity.
